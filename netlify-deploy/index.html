The Gap

The output was never the whole problem.

A model does not only answer a question. Across a long relationship it shapes what a person trusts, how they read their own situation, and which decisions feel available to them. That is infrastructure, and right now almost no framework governs it.

Emotional Infrastructure is the research and governance work built to make that influence visible and accountable. It treats the sequence, not the single response, as the thing that has to be checked.

Trajectory GovernanceGoverning AI influence across sequences of interactions, not isolated outputs or incidents.
Behavioral Signal SovereigntyUsers' right to visibility and control over how their signals are interpreted, stored, and operationalized.
Benevolent CaptureThe risk state where a system appears helpful while quietly shaping dependence and agency over time.

About

Brittany Wright

FounderEmotional Infrastructure

DisciplineCybersecurity · Digital ethics
WritingUnauthorized Inference

Brittany Wright is an independent AI governance researcher working at the intersection of cybersecurity, digital ethics, and human-centered systems.

She is the founder of Emotional Infrastructure and a Principal Investigator at the Center for Educational and Instructional Technology Research. She writes on trajectory governance, behavioral signal sovereignty, and the accountability gap in how AI influence is measured over time.

Her argument stays consistent across everything she builds. AI governance has to move past moderating discrete outputs toward genuine accountability for the sustained influence adaptive systems exert on the people who use them.
